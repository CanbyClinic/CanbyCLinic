Notice of Privacy Practices — Review Required

A HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices must accurately describe the clinic’s real uses, disclosures, duties, contacts, and patient rights.

Not a final legal notice: The clinic must publish an approved Notice of Privacy Practices that matches its actual operations. Do not substitute generic website language for the formal notice required of a covered health care provider.

What the final notice should address

  • How medical information may be used and disclosed.
  • The clinic’s duties to protect information and follow its notice.
  • Patient rights to access, amend, restrict, and receive an accounting where applicable.
  • How to request confidential communications.
  • How to file a complaint without retaliation.
  • The privacy contact, effective date, and current reproductive-health and Part 2 requirements when applicable.

Official model materials

The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services publishes model notices for covered health care providers. The clinic should adapt those materials with qualified compliance or legal review rather than copying language that may not match its practices.
