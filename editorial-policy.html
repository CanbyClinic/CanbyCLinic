Editorial Policy

How the Health Education Library Is Researched, Written, and Updated.

The goal is not to imitate a medical textbook or produce search-engine filler. Each article should help a reader understand a decision, prepare a useful question, and recognize the limits of general information.

Source Standard

Articles prioritize public-health agencies, government data, evidence-based guideline organizations, peer-reviewed research, and primary institutional sources. Sources are listed at the end of each article so readers can review the underlying material.

Medical Limits

General education cannot account for an individual’s history, medications, pregnancy status, symptoms, examination, or test results. Every article includes a visible medical-information notice and directs emergency concerns to 911.

No Invented Reviewers

The website does not claim that an article was reviewed by a named physician, nurse practitioner, or other clinician unless that person has actually reviewed the final text and authorized the attribution. The current articles identify the clinic as publisher and do not invent a medical reviewer.

Local Data

Neighborhood statistics are used to guide operational questions—not to stereotype individuals. ZIP-code and community averages are clearly described as population-level context and should not be treated as a diagnosis of any resident.

Updates and Corrections

Each article displays an updated date. Material should be revised when a source changes, a recommendation is replaced, a link stops working, or a factual error is identified. Substantive corrections should be reflected in the article rather than hidden.

Search Visibility

Titles, descriptions, headings, descriptive links, and image alt text are written to help people and search engines understand the page. Keywords are used only where they accurately describe the content. Medical claims are not added merely to attract traffic.

Images

Website images are illustrative and should not be interpreted as photographs of actual clinic staff, patients, or the Canby Avenue facility unless explicitly labeled otherwise.
