Health education

Clear information helps patients carry a plan into daily life.

What this means

Education should explain the reason for a recommendation, the steps involved, expected follow-up, and warning signs. It should not replace individual diagnosis or treatment.

Service availability, cost, eligibility, and outside referral requirements must be confirmed by phone before you rely on this information.

  • Medication safety

How to prepare

  • Ask for plain-language instructions
  • Request language assistance when needed
  • Repeat the plan in your own words
  • Use the health articles as general education, not personal medical advice
