Appointments

Call first to confirm that the clinic can address your concern, whether an appointment is available, and what you should bring.

(818) 674-4414
Safest way to start

Use the phone for medical-service questions.

This static website is not a patient portal and does not collect health information. Call the clinic to discuss appointment availability. Do not use ordinary email for symptoms, diagnoses, test results, medical records, insurance member numbers, or prescription details.

Emergency: Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency department for chest pain, severe breathing difficulty, stroke symptoms, major bleeding, loss of consciousness, or another emergency.

Before calling

  • Know the main reason you are seeking care.
  • Ask whether the service is available.
  • Ask about eligibility and expected cost.
  • Ask whether insurance or identification is needed.
  • Ask whether walk-ins are accepted or an appointment is required.

General callback request

This creates an email in your own email app. Enter only contact details. Do not include health information.

Prepare for the visit

What to bring

Medication information

Bring all medication and supplement containers, or an accurate list with names, strengths, doses, and timing.

Relevant records

Bring recent laboratory results, imaging reports, discharge instructions, referral paperwork, and contact information for other clinicians.

Identification and coverage

Ask by phone whether identification, insurance information, or income documentation is required. Do not assume.
